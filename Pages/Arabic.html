About Arabic Calligraphy

Arabic calligraphy is renowned for its fluid, intricate, and artistic forms. Its letters are primarily connected, flowing from right to left in graceful curves and loops. The Arabic alphabet consists of 28 letters, written in different forms depending on their position in a word (isolated, initial, medial, or final).

Common Scripts:

  • Naskh: A highly legible and widely used script, perfect for books and official texts. Its rounded letters and uniformity make it aesthetically pleasing.
  • Thuluth: A decorative and ornate style developed during the Byzantine era, combining flowing lines and intricate details.
  • Diwani: A cursive and highly ornamental script used in official Ottoman documents, characterized by its interwoven letters and elaborate decorations.
