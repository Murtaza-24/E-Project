Indic

About Indic Calligraphy

The diversity of Indic calligraphy reflects the rich linguistic heritage of the Indian subcontinent. Each script has distinct characteristics, and they are written from left to right, except for Urdu, which follows right-to-left writing.


    Hindi (Devanagari):
  • Alphabet Size: 47 characters (33 consonants and 14 vowels).
  • Defined by its horizontal headstroke (shirorekha) and straight, angular letters. It is often used in manuscripts and artistic depictions of verses.

    • Urdu (Nastaliq):
  • Alphabet Size: 38 letters.
  • Derived from Persian calligraphy, Nastaliq is known for its slanting style and elegant, flowing curves. It is highly aesthetic and often used in poetry and literature.

    • Bangla (Bengali):
  • Alphabet Size: 50 letters (11 vowels and 39 consonants).
  • The script is rounded, with its letters featuring loops and curves. Calligraphy in this style often highlights the natural rhythm and beauty of the Bangla language.
