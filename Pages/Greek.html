About Greek Calligraphy

Greek calligraphy reflects a long history of artistic writing, dating back to ancient Greece. The letters, based on the Greek alphabet, are symmetrical and often geometric, lending themselves to both classical and modern designs. The Greek alphabet is written from left to right and consists of 24 letters, with both uppercase and lowercase forms.

Common Styles:

  • Classical Greek: Inspired by ancient inscriptions, these letters are bold and monumental, often seen in historical art and architecture.
  • Byzantine Greek: A decorative and ornate style developed during the Byzantine era, combining flowing lines and intricate details.
  • Modern Greek Calligraphy: A blend of traditional forms with contemporary aesthetics, emphasizing elegance and simplicity.
